About

Allemansrätten is an open Point-of-Interest map to find leasure spots in public space on Belgian soil. It's a not-for-profit initiative set up by a group of citizens.


It looks like we will all be travelling in our own country this summer! While we often think of travelling as going far away, what would happen if we left to discover the unknown places around us? What if this summer could make you discover Belgium in ways you’ve never done before?

This is the perfect time to discover what Belgium is all about, from Flanders to Wallonia passing through Brussels. Adventure begins at the end of the roads we know!

Allemansrätten is a map with freely accessible leasure places in Belgium. Ranging from places where you can swim or climb, to places with public barbecues. Everyone probably knows a few of these places close to home, but when you're on the move they can be hard to find.

The name is the Scandinavian word for "Right to Roam". This is the philosophy we support: claim and optimally use nature and public space. Belgium does not have this Allemansrätten. Access to nature is restricted unless otherwise stated, swimming in lakes and rivers is not allowed unless it is allowed. This is something we would like to see changed...

This project is a derivative of Welcome to my garden. Shoutout to them for doing the initial heavy lifting of coding this page!

The source code of this project is available on GitHub. Any suggestions to make it better? Feel free to contribute!

Recommendations
Planning to discover Belgium? Here are some links and tools we'd recommend you to use!

  • Camping in someone else's garden: Welcome To My Garden
  • Route planner for cycling and hiking: BRouter
  • Great navigation app for cycling and hiking: OSMAnd
  • Overview of the whole cycling network cycling layer of OpenStreetMap
  • Long distance trails in Wallonia: le RAVeL
  • Long distance trails in Flanders: LF network Flanders
  • Hospitality network for bicycle tourists: WarmShowers
  • More detailed information about all bivouac places: Bivakzone.be
  • Check the water quality of public swimming zones (Flanders): kwaliteitzwemwater.be
  • Check the water quality of public swimming zones (Wallonia): environnement.wallonie.be/baignade
  • Geocaching - treasure hunting in public space: geocaching.com
Enjoy!

Rules
Here’s how Allemansrätten works.
Make sure to read the rules before starting your adventure!
Network of citizens
Allemansrätten is a map with freely accessible leasure places in Belgium. Ranging from places where you can swim or skate, to places with public barbecues. This is a not-for-profit initiative and no income should be made from it.

Disclaimer
Allemansrätten is a citizen initiative and only serves to collect and map publicly accessible leasure places. The information on the map is crowdsourced, as such cannnot take responsibility of the accuraccy of the data on the map. The volunteers behind Allemansrätten will not take any responsibility for any loss, damage or injury following the use of the website and the information listed.

POI removal
If a place is not relevant, accessible or should be removed for whatever reason, let us know at allemansratten.belgium[at]gmail.com

Respect for nature - Leave no trace
All your waste has to be taken away. Respect nature and the environment.
Consider doing more: take away any trash you find and help us cleanup the environment!

Noise pollution
Please be considerate of the others and your surroundings. Don't force your neighbors to tolerate whatever music you blare trough your bluetooth speaker, keep it at a reasonable level.

Follow the local rules and laws
The places on the map are considered to be publicly accessible. Local rules apply. Allemansrätten does not condone breaking any local laws and trespassing in any shape or form.

Have fun!
This is a great opportunity to explore nature and have fun! Learn a few words of french, dutch or german if you’re travelling in a different region than yours. Be curious, share stories and make new friendships!

Spread the word about Allemansrätten
